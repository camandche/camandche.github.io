Reflections over Foundations Sprints

Empathetic, Self-Awareness Values

Life's journey, has given me the opportunity to be able to evaluate myself by soulfully checking in, asking: "Am I ok?"" If yes, then keep doing what you love, otherwise if not, what's stopping you from following your dreams & how can you get over this particular hurdle? Life's too short; enjoy it while you can!

We are unique individuals that have to get along with loved ones as well as your peers or colleagues. So how can a shift in your attitude make things better?

Being a social-butterfly, I enjoy being around other people. Upon reflection, I discovered I 'people-watch'! Just love watching how we interact with others. Humans are such fascinating creatures! I can easily socialise with others, being present in a conversation & being mindful of other people's thoughts & feelings around me. Can't explain how & when this happened, but I've always been able to gauge a person's character from both their spoken language while also observing their body language (that can say something else) as well. Then there's other times where I just need some time out alone to gather my thoughts to re-center & have a chance to heal.

This lead me to understand the difference between Growth vs Fixed Mindsets & EQ vs IQ. I definitely need to expand my mind, constantly searching to fill it with more knowledge; just love being a sponge! 'm more in tune with my Emotional Quotient (EQ) self but I toggle into my intellectual (IQ) self often too. Found it weird that EDA really focused on these areas, but understand where they're coming from & why they're wanting us to develop an understanding here. I did find the core learning challenging in the sense that I came into this training already having an understanding about this & I would have preferred us to have the option to opt out so that I could spend more time focusing on the programming aspects instead. But in saying that, found 'neuroplasticity' quite interesting in the end.

Having an entreuprenual vibe, I already knew that I do things differently to the normal. My visions tend to be quite extreme where people can't see the end-goal until it's right there in front of them. I'm one of those people at the moment where I kow where I want to be but can't see how to get there. But I'm a go-getter, where I'll set myself a target to achieve & will somehow find a way to reach this goal.

Since starting this course, I've had the fingers crossed that things will all make sense somehow. It's the drive that's kept me here having a sense of upskill yourself & utilise your smarts to do something you're passionate about.

My ego is usually in check & never really explodes too large, unless I'm in a comfortable environment & my singing takes centre-stage. In relation to learning & programming, my ego needs to take a back-seat as I need to reach out more to either my facilitators, or our Slack Community as soon as I get stuck just to help me cross the line with a better understanding of things.

Bootcamp Expectations

Long Term Goal & Career Path

Confidently being able to hold my ground as a well rounded programmer, being able to work together as a team & to also be able to take this skillset anywhere around the world & work hours that are suitably adjustable for my other interests in my realm.

Biggest Strengths & Limitations

Compared my thoughts from Sprint 2 to now; still the same. I have numerous interests that I would love to surround myself with daily. I seem to be able to multi-task a little better now that I have a few more weeks settled in however, silence is still my 'go-to' throughout the day & noticed my brick wall ego still exists. It's weird as I am literally scared of programming, yet it is something that I am drawn to still; how does that work?! Must be that 'Growth Mindset' going on!

Biggest Non-Technical Challenge

Not being physically together in the same location as my cohort

Non-Techncial Skills - Human Skills

Teaming up with the Peer Programming. Hoping this adjustment will be beneficial for myself as well as my peers

Bootcamp Expectations

Wanting to be able to put my hand up in a timely manner in order to be able to keep up with the rest of the cohort. I'm wanting to have the opportunity to learn & grow with confidence in programming
